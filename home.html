Let's give some love to an animal in need!

Some sad stuff...

  • An estimated 167,388 breeding dogs are currently living in United States Department of Agriculture (USDA)-licensed commercial facilities for breeding purposes this very moment.
  • There are an estimated 10,000 puppy mills in the United States (this includes both licensed and unlicensed facilities).
  • Over 2 million puppies are bred in mills each year.
  • An estimated 1.2 million dogs are euthanized in shelters every year.

But you can change that!

  • On the plus side, there are about 1.2 million dogs each year that would love to find a happy home!
  • You can also be a long-term or short-term puppy foster parent. Sometimes, pets are too young to be placed in a shelter; they need a clean, safe, environment to help them prepare to find a new forever home!
  • Studies have shown that just petting a dog can lower the petter's blood pressure and heart rate (while having a positive effect on the dog as well).

Even if you can't adopt or foster a puppy right now (or ever), there are still some great ways you can help!

Lots of shelters and animal control centers are in need of volunteers; this usually only requires a few hours of time each month, and it basically means you get to play with animals for free! Shelter animals need exercise, attention, and socialization.

Don't have time to volunteer, but still want to help?

Your local shelter would appreciate any donation you can make! Even a dollar would help to pay for an animal to be spayed, vaccinated, and cared for until it can find a loving home. All donations are tax-deductible.

Here are some great places to go:
  • Petfinder and Adopt a Pet will help you find local shelters in your area.
  • New York Animal Control Center has some great opportunities for volunteering with the program or adopting animals.
